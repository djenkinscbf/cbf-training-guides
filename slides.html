Overview

What is Google Slides?

Google Slides is CBF's tool for creating presentations — from internal project updates and team briefings to external donor reports, board presentations, and partner workshops. Every CBF presentation should reflect the organisation's brand identity consistently.

How CBF uses Google Slides

Slides is used for board meetings and governance presentations, donor and partner briefings, project launch and close-out presentations, training sessions and workshops, and all-staff communications. CBF has an official PowerPoint template that can be imported into Google Slides — always start from the CBF template, not a blank slide or a generic Google theme.

Getting Started

Creating a new presentation

Always start from the CBF template. This ensures every presentation is on-brand from the first slide and saves significant formatting time.

1
Start from the CBF template
  1. 1
    Locate the CBF PowerPoint Template (.pptx) in the team's Shared Drive — it is stored in the CBF Brand Assets folder. Ask your manager or the Communications team if you cannot find it
  2. 2
    Right-click the template file → Open with → Google Slides. This opens it in Slides format while preserving all layouts and branding
  3. 3
    Immediately click File → Make a copy and rename your copy — example: 2026_BoardMeeting_Q2Update_DRAFT. Move it to the correct project folder in Shared Drive
  4. 4
    Never edit the original template file. Always work from a copy
Template first, always: Creating a presentation from a blank Google Slide or a generic theme produces an off-brand result. Every CBF presentation that leaves the organisation — to donors, partners, or the board — must use the CBF template. No exceptions.
2
Navigate the Slides interface
  1. 1
    The slide panel on the left shows thumbnail previews of all slides — click any thumbnail to jump to that slide
  2. 2
    The editing canvas in the centre is where you work on the selected slide
  3. 3
    The toolbar at the top contains formatting options — text, shapes, images, lines, and more
  4. 4
    Right-click anywhere in the slide panel to add, duplicate, delete, or reorder slides
CBF Brand Standards

Applying CBF branding in Slides

Every CBF presentation communicates the organisation's identity to its audience. Consistent, correct use of brand elements — colours, fonts, logo, and motifs — is not optional. It reflects CBF's credibility and professionalism.

3
Use the correct CBF colours

Apply CBF's colour palette using the 60-30-10 rule: 60% primary colours (blue/green), 30% neutrals (white/grey), 10% accent colours (saffron, teal, persimmon, raspberry).

Adding CBF colours to Google Slides When selecting a fill or font colour in Slides, click Custom at the bottom of the colour picker and enter the HEX code directly (e.g. #1F4A94). Save custom colours to your palette for faster access on future slides.
Never create unofficial colour variations: Do not use lighter or darker tints of CBF colours, or mix in non-brand colours. The hex codes above are the only approved values. This rule applies to all CBF presentations regardless of audience.
4
Use correct CBF typography
  1. 1
    CBF's official typeface is Outfit. Use Outfit ExtraBold for slide titles and section headers, and Outfit Light or Regular for body text and captions
  2. 2
    To install Outfit in Google Slides: click the font name dropdown in the toolbar → click More fonts → search "Outfit" → click it to add it to your font list
  3. 3
    If Outfit is unavailable on a specific device, use Arial as the substitute — it is the approved fallback per the CBF Brand Toolkit
  4. 4
    Keep text concise — maximum 8–10 words per line on a slide. Slides are not documents. If you need more text, split it across two slides
Typography hierarchy for slides: H1 (slide title): 36–44px ExtraBold · H2 (section header): 28–32px Bold · Body text: 18–20px Regular or Light · Captions/labels: 14–16px Regular. Scale down slightly for dense content slides.
5
Logo placement and usage rules
  1. 1
    The CBF logo must appear on the cover slide and the closing/contact slide at minimum. It may also appear on section divider slides
  2. 2
    Place the logo in a clear area — always on a solid background. If the background is dark (navy or green), use the white version of the logo. On light backgrounds, use the full-colour version
  3. 3
    Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the width of the letter "C" in the CBF wordmark. Do not crowd it with text or other elements
  4. 4
    Never stretch, rotate, recolour, or recreate the logo. Always use the official logo files from the CBF Brand Assets folder in Shared Drive
Logo on every content slide is not required: The CBF template places the logo on key slides. You do not need the logo on every single content slide — that clutters the layout. The template handles logo placement correctly — trust it.
Slide Design

Building effective slides

A CBF presentation should be visual, clear, and structured. Slides support what you say — they do not replace it. Less text, more impact.

6
Structure your slide deck

Every CBF presentation should follow this structure, using the corresponding layouts from the CBF template:

Cover slide
Presentation title, subtitle, date, and CBF logo. Use the template cover layout. No bullet points or body text here.
Agenda / Contents
List the main sections — keep to 4–6 items maximum. Gives the audience orientation from the start.
Section dividers
Use the template's divider layout between major sections. Bold section title only — no body text on divider slides.
Content slides
One key idea per slide. Title + visuals or 3–5 bullet points maximum. Never more than 8–10 words per line.
Data / impact slides
Use large, bold numbers for key figures. Charts and icons from Sheets. Let the numbers do the talking.
Closing slide
CBF logo, contact information, and website. End with the CBF mission statement or a key call to action. Never end on a blank slide.
7
Add and format text boxes
  1. 1
    Click on a text placeholder from the template to edit it — or click Insert → Text box and draw a new text area on the slide
  2. 2
    Keep bullet points to 3–5 items per slide maximum. Each bullet should be a fragment, not a full sentence — you will speak the full detail aloud
  3. 3
    To adjust line spacing: select the text → Format → Line & paragraph spacing. Use 1.15–1.5 spacing for readability on screen
  4. 4
    Align text to the left or centre for most slide content. Avoid justified text — it creates uneven spacing that is harder to read at presentation sizes
8
Slide design: do's and don'ts
✓ Do
✓One key idea per slide — split complex content across multiple slides
✓Use high-quality Caribbean imagery — full-bleed where possible
✓Use large bold numbers for impact statistics
✓Leave generous white space — slides should breathe
✓Use CBF wave, dot ripple, or leaf motifs as subtle decorative accents
✓Keep the slide title to 6 words or fewer
✕ Don't
✕Put entire paragraphs of text on a slide — this is a document, not a presentation
✕Use non-CBF colours, fonts, or generic Google themes
✕Crowd a slide with multiple charts, images, and text blocks at once
✕Use more than 2–3 different font sizes on one slide
✕Use low-resolution or non-Caribbean imagery
✕Mix too many accent colours — pick one accent per slide
Visual Content

Images, charts, and shapes

Strong visuals are a core part of CBF's brand identity. Photography, charts from Google Sheets, and brand icons and motifs all have their place in CBF presentations.

9
Insert and format images
  1. 1
    Click Insert → Image → choose your source: upload from computer, search the web, Google Drive, or Google Photos
  2. 2
    CBF imagery should depict Caribbean landscapes, marine life, people, and ecosystems — always high-resolution, authentic, and optimistic in tone. See the CBF Brand Toolkit imagery guidelines for full guidance
  3. 3
    To resize while maintaining proportions: hold Shift while dragging a corner handle. To crop an image: right-click → Crop image and drag the crop handles
  4. 4
    For photo mask effects (circular or wave-cropped images per the CBF Brand Toolkit): right-click the image → Mask image → choose a shape. Round (circle) masks work well for people and community shots
Image quality: Never use images that appear blurry or pixelated on the slide canvas — they will look worse when projected. Use images at least 1920×1080px for full-slide visuals. Check the CBF shared photo library in Drive for approved, high-resolution images.
10
Embed a live chart from Google Sheets
  1. 1
    Open the Google Sheet containing your chart → click the chart → click the three-dot menu on the chart → Copy chart
  2. 2
    Switch to your Google Slides presentation → paste (Ctrl+V). A dialog asks whether to Link to spreadsheet or paste unlinked — always choose Link to spreadsheet
  3. 3
    When the underlying Sheet data changes, a Update button appears on the chart in Slides — click it to refresh the chart with the latest figures
Live data in presentations: Linking charts from Sheets means your donor and board presentations always reflect the most current project data. No more manually updating numbers slide by slide before every meeting.
11
Add shapes, icons, and CBF motifs
  1. 1
    Click Insert → Shape to add geometric shapes — use rounded rectangles for callout boxes and labels, echoing the circular language of the CBF logo
  2. 2
    CBF brand motifs (waves, dot ripples, leaf shapes) are available as SVG files in the CBF Brand Assets folder in Shared Drive. Insert them via Insert → Image → Drive and position them as subtle decorative elements in corners or edges
  3. 3
    Use one motif per slide maximum — waves for marine/coastal content, leaves for terrestrial/nature content, dots for general brand texture. Never layer multiple motifs on one slide
  4. 4
    To align multiple objects: select them all (Shift+click each) → right-click → Align & distribute → choose centre, left, right, top, or bottom alignment
Transitions & Animations

Transitions and animations

Used well, transitions and animations guide the audience's attention. Used poorly, they are a distraction. CBF presentations should feel polished and professional — not flashy.

12
Add slide transitions
  1. 1
    Click View → Transition (or right-click a slide thumbnail → Change transition) to open the Transitions panel
  2. 2
    Choose a transition style — for CBF presentations use Fade or Slide from right for a clean, professional feel. Set the speed to Medium
  3. 3
    Click Apply to all slides to use the same transition throughout — consistency looks more professional than mixing transition styles
CBF standard: Use Fade transitions at Medium speed for all formal CBF presentations (board, donor, partner). Reserve livelier transitions only for internal training or workshop sessions where a lighter tone is appropriate.
13
Add object animations
  1. 1
    Click an object on the slide (text box, image, shape) → click View → Animate to open the Animations panel
  2. 2
    Click Add animation → choose the animation type (Appear, Fade in, or Fly in from bottom are clean choices for CBF decks) and set timing: On click lets you control the pace, After previous runs automatically
  3. 3
    Use animations to reveal bullet points one at a time on content-heavy slides — this keeps the audience focused on what you are currently discussing rather than reading ahead
Less is more: Spinning, bouncing, or complex animations are not appropriate for formal CBF presentations. If in doubt, use no animation at all — a clean static slide is always preferable to a distracting one.
Presenting

Presenting your slides

Google Slides has several presentation modes — including a presenter view with speaker notes that only you can see. Use these tools to deliver polished, confident presentations.

14
Start and navigate a presentation
  1. 1
    Click the Slideshow button (top-right, play icon) or press Ctrl+F5 to start the presentation from the current slide. Press Ctrl+Shift+F5 to start from the beginning
  2. 2
    Navigate slides with the arrow keys, spacebar (forward), or click the on-screen arrows. Press Esc to exit the slideshow at any time
  3. 3
    To jump to a specific slide: type the slide number and press Enter during the presentation
  4. 4
    Press B during a presentation to black out the screen — useful when you want the audience to focus on discussion rather than the slide
15
Use Presenter View with speaker notes
  1. 1
    Click the dropdown arrow next to the Slideshow button → select Presenter view. This opens a separate window showing your notes, a slide timer, and a preview of the next slide
  2. 2
    The audience sees only the presentation on the main screen — you see your speaker notes on your own screen. Move the presenter window to your laptop screen and the main presentation to the projector or external display
  3. 3
    To add speaker notes: click the Click to add speaker notes area below the slide canvas in the editor — type your talking points here. Keep notes concise — keywords, not scripts
Always use Presenter View for formal meetings: Speaker notes let you glance at key statistics, names, or talking points without breaking eye contact with your audience. This is particularly valuable for board and donor presentations.
16
Present via Google Meet
  1. 1
    In Google Slides, click Slideshow → Present to a meeting — this connects directly to your active Google Meet session without needing to screen share separately
  2. 2
    Alternatively, in Google Meet click Present now → A tab and select your Slides browser tab
  3. 3
    Participants see your slides in real time. You retain control of advancing slides from your own screen
Before any virtual presentation: Do a dry run — open the presentation, enter Presenter View, and go through every slide checking for typos, broken chart links, and missing images. A 10-minute rehearsal prevents the most common presentation problems.
Working Together

Collaborating on presentations

Multiple people can work on the same presentation simultaneously in Google Slides — useful for large team decks or when sections are owned by different staff members.

17
Comment on specific slides
  1. 1
    Click on the slide or object you want to comment on → press Ctrl+Alt+M or click Insert → Comment
  2. 2
    Type your feedback and use @name to tag a specific colleague. They receive an email with a direct link to the slide and comment
  3. 3
    Resolve comments once addressed. Before a presentation is finalised, all comments should be resolved
Review process for formal decks: For board or donor presentations, share the deck in Commenter access with your manager or the Communications team before finalising. This ensures brand compliance and content accuracy before the presentation reaches its audience.
Exporting

Exporting and sharing presentations

Export your presentation in the appropriate format depending on how it will be used and who will receive it.

18
Export to PDF or PowerPoint
File→ Download→ Select format
  1. 1
    PDF (.pdf) — for sending to external audiences (donors, partners, board members). The layout is locked and renders identically on all devices. Use for final versions only
  2. 2
    PowerPoint (.pptx) — when a partner or donor requests an editable version, or when the file will be opened in Microsoft Office. Note that some Google Slides features may not render perfectly in PowerPoint
  3. 3
    PNG or JPEG images — export individual slides as images for use in reports, social media, or email newsletters via File → Download → PNG image
Archive final presentations to Zoho WorkDrive Once a presentation has been delivered and is final — board meeting slides, donor briefings, project close-out decks — export as PDF and archive to the correct folder in Zoho WorkDrive. This ensures CBF's institutional knowledge is preserved. The Google Slides file remains in Drive for future reference and adaptation.

Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Habits that make your presentations more effective, on-brand, and professional.

One idea per slide

If a slide needs a scroll bar to read, it has too much content. Split it across two slides. Your audience reads faster than you speak — keep them on the same point as you.

Get brand approval for external decks

Before any presentation goes to a donor, partner, or the board, share it with the Communications team for a brand review. Especially for first-time presenters or new templates.

Link your data, don't copy it

Embed charts from Google Sheets with a live link. When project data updates, your presentation stays current with one click — no manual reformatting required.

Use speaker notes, not slide text

Everything you plan to say that doesn't fit on the slide goes in speaker notes. Your slides should be a visual anchor — not a transcript. Notes are visible only to you in Presenter View.

Archive delivered presentations

Export as PDF and upload to Zoho WorkDrive after every formal presentation. This builds CBF's institutional memory and makes future presentations easier to build on.

Rehearse before presenting

Go through the full deck in Presenter View before any formal presentation. Check all linked charts are updated, no slides are missing, and your speaker notes are complete.
