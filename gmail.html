Overview

What is Gmail?

Gmail is CBF's official email platform, part of Google Workspace. All staff communicate internally and externally through their CBF Gmail account — it is your primary professional communication channel.

Your CBF email address

Your CBF Gmail address follows the format firstnameinitiallastname@caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org. For example, David Brown would be dbrown@caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org. This address is used for all CBF business — project communications, partner correspondence, and internal coordination.

Getting Started

Accessing your Gmail

You can access CBF Gmail from any web browser or via the Gmail mobile app. Sign in with your CBF credentials — never use a personal Google account for CBF business.

1
Sign in via web browser
  1. 1
    Open any browser and go to mail.google.com or gmail.com
  2. 2
    Enter your CBF email: firstnameinitiallastname@caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org — for example, dbrown@caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org
  3. 3
    Enter your password. If this is your first login, use the temporary password provided by IT and change it immediately
  4. 4
    Complete any two-factor authentication (2FA) steps if prompted
Important: Always sign out of Gmail when using a shared or public computer. Never check "Keep me signed in" on devices that aren't yours.
2
Set up the Gmail mobile app
  1. 1
    Download the Gmail app from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
  2. 2
    Tap Add an email address → Select Google
  3. 3
    Enter your CBF email and password to sign in
  4. 4
    If you also have a personal Gmail, always confirm you are sending from your CBF account — check the "From" field before sending
Tip: On mobile, tap your profile icon (top right) to switch between accounts. Always confirm you're on your CBF account before composing work emails.
Core Tasks

Composing & sending emails

Learn to compose, reply, forward, and send emails efficiently — including formatting, attachments, and scheduling.

3
Compose a new email
  1. 1
    Click the Compose button (bottom-left, pencil icon) to open a new message window
  2. 2
    In the To field, start typing a name — Gmail will suggest CBF contacts from the directory automatically
  3. 3
    Use Cc to include others who need to be informed, and Bcc when recipients should not see each other's addresses (e.g. bulk announcements)
  4. 4
    Write a clear, descriptive Subject line — keep it under 60 characters and flag the action needed where relevant (e.g. ACTION NEEDED: Q2 Report Review by May 15)
  5. 5
    Write your message, then click the blue Send button — or use the arrow next to it to Schedule send for a specific date and time
Good subject line habits: Use prefixes like ACTION NEEDED, FYI, DECISION REQUIRED, or RESPONSE BY [DATE] at the start of your subject when relevant. This helps recipients prioritise.
4
Reply and forward emails
  1. 1
    Open an email and click Reply (arrow icon at the bottom) to respond only to the sender
  2. 2
    Click Reply All (two arrows) to respond to all recipients — use this thoughtfully to avoid unnecessary inbox clutter for others
  3. 3
    Click Forward to send the thread to someone new — always add context at the top explaining why you are forwarding
  4. 4
    To undo a sent email: click Undo in the notification bar at the bottom — you have up to 30 seconds depending on your settings
Extend your undo window: Go to Settings → General → Undo Send → set the cancellation period to 30 seconds for maximum time to catch mistakes.
5
Attach files and insert links
  1. 1
    To attach a file from your computer: click the paperclip icon at the bottom of the compose window and select the file
  2. 2
    To share a Google Drive file: click the Drive icon (triangle) in the compose toolbar — this inserts a shareable link rather than a copy, preferable for large or frequently updated files
  3. 3
    To insert a hyperlink: select the text, click the chain/link icon in the formatting toolbar, and paste the URL
Best practice: For documents shared widely or updated regularly, always share via Google Drive link. This ensures recipients always see the latest version.
Inbox Management

Organising your inbox

A well-organised inbox helps you stay on top of CBF's fast-moving workload. Use labels, stars, and filters to keep things manageable.

6
Using labels to organise email

Labels in Gmail work like folders but are more powerful — an email can have multiple labels at once.

  1. 1
    In the left sidebar, scroll down and click + Create new label
  2. 2
    Name your label (e.g. Projects/Reef Resilience, Finance, Partners) — use forward slashes to create nested sub-labels
  3. 3
    To apply a label to an email: open the email, click the label icon at the top, and select the relevant label(s)
  4. 4
    Right-click a label in the sidebar to change its colour for quick visual identification
7
Creating filters to auto-organise email
Settings ⚙→ See all settings→ Filters and Blocked Addresses→ Create a new filter
  1. 1
    Enter criteria: sender address, subject keywords, or recipient. Click Create filter
  2. 2
    Choose an action: Apply label, Skip Inbox (archive), Mark as read, or Star it
  3. 3
    Check Also apply filter to matching conversations to apply retroactively, then click Create filter
Example: Filter all emails from @caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org → apply label "Internal" so internal emails are always visually distinct from external correspondence.
8
Using stars and snooze to manage follow-ups
  1. 1
    Star an email by clicking the star icon next to it — starred emails appear in the Starred folder in the left sidebar
  2. 2
    Snooze an email by hovering over it and clicking the clock icon — choose when it should reappear at the top of your inbox
  3. 3
    Use Google Tasks integration: open an email and click Add to Tasks (tick icon in the right panel) to turn it into an action item with a due date
Professional Identity

Setting up your email signature

Your CBF email signature should be set up when you join and kept current. You can update it at any time directly in Gmail Settings to reflect changes to your name, title, phone number, or other details.

9
Adding or updating your signature
Settings ⚙→ See all settings→ General tab→ Signature section
  1. 1
    Scroll down to the Signature section and click + Create new (or click an existing signature name to edit it)
  2. 2
    Name it CBF Official. Enter your details — your name, job title, department, phone number, and the CBF website. Use the formatting toolbar to apply consistent styling
  3. 3
    Under Signature defaults, set this signature to appear on both New emails and Replies/forwards
  4. 4
    Scroll to the bottom and click Save Changes. Your signature will now automatically appear in all outgoing emails
Keeping it current: If your title, phone number, or team changes, update your signature directly in Settings at any time. Keep it professional and consistent with CBF's communication standards.
Shared Access

Delegated access & shared inboxes

Some CBF roles involve managing shared inboxes (e.g. info@, secretariat@). Delegation allows you to read and send from these inboxes without sharing passwords — shared inbox access is managed by your direct manager.

10
Accessing a delegated inbox

Delegation is set up by your manager. Once granted, you can access the shared inbox directly from your own account — no separate login required.

  1. 1
    Click your profile icon (top-right corner of Gmail). If delegation has been granted, the shared inbox name will appear in the dropdown
  2. 2
    Click the shared inbox name to switch to it — you are now viewing and managing that inbox on behalf of the account
  3. 3
    Emails sent from a delegated inbox display the shared address as the sender, maintaining a professional appearance
Never share your password to give someone inbox access. Delegation is the correct and secure method. Contact your manager to set up delegation properly.
11
Setting up an out-of-office (vacation responder)
Settings ⚙→ See all settings→ General tab→ Vacation responder
  1. 1
    Toggle Vacation responder on and set your First day and Last day
  2. 2
    Write a professional message that includes: your return date, who to contact in your absence (with their email), and whether you have limited email access
  3. 3
    Check Only send a response to people in my Contacts to avoid auto-replying to newsletters or spam. Click Save Changes
Best practice: Set your out-of-office at least one business day before leave. Inform your supervisor and active project contacts directly, in addition to the auto-reply.
CBF Policies

Important policies & security rules

These rules protect CBF's communications, data, and reputation. All staff are expected to follow them without exception.

Non-negotiable rules
✕Never share your password. Not with colleagues, managers, or IT support. If someone needs inbox access, request delegated access through your manager.
✕Never use your CBF email for personal purposes. Your CBF account is for CBF business only — do not subscribe to personal mailing lists, sign up for personal services, or use it as a recovery email for personal accounts.
✕Never forward confidential CBF communications to personal email accounts. If you need remote access, sign into Gmail via browser using your CBF account.
✕Never click suspicious links or download unexpected attachments. If an email looks unusual — even from a known CBF contact — verify with the sender before clicking.
✕Never send sensitive personal data (IDs, financial information, passwords) via email unless through an encrypted channel approved by your manager.
Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Small habits that make a big difference in keeping CBF's communications clear, professional, and efficient.

Keep emails concise

Aim for emails under 200 words. If the topic is complex, schedule a Google Meet instead. Use bullet points rather than long paragraphs.

Respond within 24 hours

Even if just to acknowledge receipt and give a timeline. Silence is unprofessional and can stall project work.

Use threads, not new emails

Reply within an existing thread rather than starting a new email on the same topic. This keeps context together and is easier to search later.

Search before you scroll

Use Gmail's search bar with operators like from:, subject:, and has:attachment to find emails instantly.

Enable 2-factor authentication

If not already enforced by IT, enable 2FA under My Account → Security. It is your strongest protection against account compromise.

Use @mentions in Docs, not email

For quick back-and-forth on a specific document, use @mention in Google Docs comments rather than sending separate emails about it.
