Overview

What is NotebookLM?

NotebookLM is a free AI-powered research and synthesis tool from Google. It lets you upload documents — reports, PDFs, papers, meeting notes — and then ask questions, request summaries, and extract insights directly from your own source material. Unlike general AI tools, NotebookLM only answers from the documents you upload, making it highly reliable for document-heavy work like CBF's grantee report reviews.

How CBF uses NotebookLM

CBF staff use NotebookLM primarily to review grantee and project reports — uploading PDFs and asking analytical questions to extract key findings, flag gaps, and prepare for review meetings. It works across multiple languages, which is particularly valuable for CBF's Caribbean-wide portfolio where reports arrive in English, Spanish, and French. Documents must be in PDF format before uploading — this is the only preparation step required.

Getting Started

Accessing NotebookLM

NotebookLM is free and accessible directly through your CBF Google account — no separate installation or subscription needed.

1
Sign in to NotebookLM
  1. 1
    Go to notebooklm.google.com in any browser
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account — use your firstnameinitiallastname@caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org address
  3. 3
    Your NotebookLM home screen shows all your notebooks — each notebook is a separate workspace containing its own set of documents and conversation history
Use your CBF account, not personal: Always access NotebookLM through your CBF Google account. This keeps CBF documents and analysis within the organisation's account ecosystem and ensures documents are not mixed with personal material.
2
Convert documents to PDF before uploading

NotebookLM requires documents to be in PDF format. This is the one preparation step before you can begin. Here is how to convert from the most common formats CBF staff encounter:

  1. 1
    From Google Docs: File → Download → PDF Document (.pdf)
  2. 2
    From Google Sheets or Slides: File → Download → PDF Document (.pdf)
  3. 3
    From Microsoft Word (.docx): File → Save As → PDF, or open in Google Docs and then download as PDF
  4. 4
    Already a PDF: No conversion needed — upload directly
Most grantee reports arrive as PDFs and can be uploaded directly without any conversion. For internal CBF documents in Google Docs format, the conversion takes under 30 seconds via File → Download → PDF.
Core Concepts

Creating and organising notebooks

A notebook is a self-contained workspace. Each notebook holds its own set of uploaded documents and its own conversation history. Think of each notebook as a dedicated research space for a specific project, report cycle, or topic area.

3
Create a new notebook
  1. 1
    On the NotebookLM home screen, click + New Notebook
  2. 2
    Give the notebook a clear, descriptive name — for example: EbA_Facility_AnnualReports_2026 or MarineConservation_GranteeReviews_Q2
  3. 3
    The notebook opens ready for you to add sources. You will see an empty sources panel on the left and a chat panel on the right
Name notebooks clearly: You may build up many notebooks over time across different programmes and cycles. Including the programme area, report type, and year in the notebook name makes them easy to find and distinguishes between cycles.
Suggested notebook types for CBF
Grantee report review
One notebook per grantee or reporting cycle. Upload all reports for that grantee to review progress and compare across periods.
Sources: Annual reports, progress reports, financial summaries
Programme area research
Load scientific papers, policy documents, and CBF reports for a programme area — marine, terrestrial, climate resilience, or SIDS.
Sources: Research papers, CBF strategy docs, IUCN reports, CBD publications
Board meeting preparation
Upload board papers before each meeting to generate concise summaries, identify key decisions, and prepare informed questions.
Sources: Board pack PDFs, previous minutes, financial reports, programme updates
Project documentation
Centralise all documents for an active project — proposals, workplans, progress reports — and query across the full project history.
Sources: Project proposals, M&E reports, workplans, site visit notes
Core Tasks

Adding sources to a notebook

Sources are the documents NotebookLM reads and reasons from. It will only answer questions based on what is in your sources — it does not draw on outside knowledge. This makes it highly reliable and accurate for CBF's specific documents.

4
Upload PDF documents
  1. 1
    Inside a notebook, click + Add source in the Sources panel on the left
  2. 2
    Select Upload → choose your PDF file(s) from your computer or Google Drive. You can upload multiple PDFs at once
  3. 3
    NotebookLM processes each document — this takes a few seconds to a minute depending on file size. A green tick appears when the source is ready
  4. 4
    Each source appears by filename in the Sources panel. Click any source to view an auto-generated summary NotebookLM has created from it
Source limit: Each notebook can hold up to 50 sources. For CBF's typical grantee review workflows — a handful of reports per cycle — you will rarely approach this limit. For large multi-country programme reviews, consider separate notebooks per region or funding cycle.
5
Add sources from Google Drive or web links
  1. 1
    Click + Add source → select Google Drive to browse and import files directly from your CBF Shared Drive — the most efficient route for reports already stored in Drive
  2. 2
    Select Link (URL) to add a public webpage as a source — useful for published research papers, IUCN resources, CBD reports, or CBF's own website content
  3. 3
    Select Copied text to paste text directly — useful for meeting notes, email summaries, or excerpts not available as a file
Import directly from Google Drive: Reports stored in CBF's Shared Drive can be added to NotebookLM without downloading and re-uploading. Browse to the file, select it, and it is added as a source instantly — saving time especially when working with large report libraries.
Core Tasks

Querying your documents

Once your sources are loaded, ask NotebookLM questions in plain language — as if consulting a knowledgeable colleague who has thoroughly read every document in the notebook. Every answer includes citations linking back to the exact source and page.

6
Ask questions in the chat
  1. 1
    Type your question in the chat panel on the right side of the notebook and press Enter
  2. 2
    NotebookLM responds with an answer drawn exclusively from your uploaded sources. Every response includes inline citations — click any citation to jump directly to the relevant passage in the source document
  3. 3
    Ask follow-up questions to dig deeper — NotebookLM maintains the context of your conversation throughout the session
  4. 4
    To start a fresh line of questioning: click New conversation at the top of the chat panel. Previous conversations are saved and accessible
Citations are the key advantage: Unlike general AI tools, NotebookLM tells you exactly where every piece of information comes from — which document, which section. This makes it trustworthy for professional use at CBF, where accuracy and accountability in reporting matter.
7
Effective questions for CBF report reviews

The quality of your questions determines the quality of the output. These question patterns are tailored to CBF's most common document review scenarios:

Progress & results
What key results has the grantee reported against their targets? Are there any indicators that are off track?
Financial review
Summarise the budget expenditure reported. Are there any significant variances between planned and actual spend?
Challenges & risks
What challenges or risks has the grantee identified? What mitigation actions have they taken or proposed?
Cross-report comparison
Compare the progress reported in the Year 1 and Year 2 reports. What has improved and what concerns remain?
Meeting preparation
Based on these reports, generate 5 questions I should ask the grantee in our upcoming review meeting.
Executive summary
Write a concise 3-paragraph summary of this report suitable for sharing with CBF senior management.
8
Save important responses to notes
  1. 1
    When NotebookLM gives a useful response, click the Save to note button (bookmark icon) on the response — this saves it to the Notes panel
  2. 2
    Notes accumulate in the Notes panel — they are preserved between sessions and can be edited, formatted, and reorganised freely
  3. 3
    To compile your notes into a structured document: click Synthesise notes — NotebookLM weaves your saved notes into a coherent draft you can copy directly into Google Docs
Build your review document as you go: Save key findings, financial observations, and flagged concerns as notes throughout your review session. At the end, use Synthesise notes to generate a structured review document — significantly faster than writing from scratch after reading a long report.
CBF Workflows

Step-by-step: Reviewing a grantee report

This is CBF's primary NotebookLM workflow — already in use by programme staff for reviewing grantee and project reports. Follow these steps for any structured report review.

1

Prepare your PDF

If the grantee report is not already a PDF, convert it first. In Google Docs: File → Download → PDF. For Word documents: open in Google Docs → download as PDF. Most grantee reports arrive as PDFs and can be uploaded directly without any conversion.

2

Create a dedicated notebook

Go to notebooklm.google.com → New Notebook. Name it clearly — for example: EbA_Facility_GranteeX_2026_Review. A dedicated notebook per grantee or reporting cycle keeps your work organised and easy to return to.

3

Upload the report and any supporting documents

Add the main report PDF. Where available, also add previous reports, the logframe, workplan, or budget summary. Multiple documents allow NotebookLM to cross-reference and identify patterns across the grantee's full reporting history.

4

Start with a broad summary

Ask: "Provide a structured summary of this report covering: overall progress, key achievements, budget status, challenges, and next steps." This gives you a high-level orientation of the report before diving into specific areas of concern.

5

Ask targeted analytical questions

Follow up with specific questions based on CBF's review criteria — indicator performance against logframe targets, budget variances, risk flags, environmental and social safeguards, and stakeholder engagement. Save key responses as notes as you go.

6

Generate follow-up questions for the grantee

Ask: "Based on this report, what are the 5 most important clarifying questions I should raise with the grantee?" This is particularly useful when preparing for a review meeting, a site visit, or a written query to the grantee.

7

Compile your notes into a review document

Once your review is complete, click Synthesise notes to generate a structured review document from your saved notes. Copy it into Google Docs, refine it, and save the final version to Zoho WorkDrive as the official programme record.

Multilingual Capability

Working across languages

One of NotebookLM's most powerful features for CBF is its multilingual capability. You can upload documents in any language and ask questions in a different language — receiving answers in your preferred language. This is particularly valuable for CBF's Caribbean-wide portfolio where documents arrive in English, Spanish, and French.

9
Query documents across languages

CBF staff have found this feature especially useful when reviewing reports submitted in Spanish or French — you can ask your questions in English and receive a full response in English, or ask in Spanish and receive answers in Spanish regardless of the document's original language.

Unique Feature

Audio Overview — Listen to your documents

NotebookLM's Audio Overview feature converts your notebook sources into a conversational audio discussion — two AI hosts discuss and explain the key content from your documents as a podcast-style summary. Useful for getting oriented in a long document before a meeting.

10
Generate an Audio Overview
  1. 1
    With sources loaded in your notebook, click the Audio Overview button → click Generate
  2. 2
    NotebookLM generates a 10–20 minute audio discussion summarising the key themes, findings, and ideas from your uploaded documents. Generation takes 1–3 minutes
  3. 3
    Press play to listen directly in the browser — you can also download the audio file to listen offline or on mobile during travel
  4. 4
    Click Customise before generating to specify what aspects to focus on or what audience the summary is for — for example, focusing on financial performance or environmental outcomes
When to use Audio Overview at CBF: Best for getting a quick orientation of a lengthy board paper or project report before a meeting, or listening to a document summary while travelling between sites. It is not a replacement for detailed review — but an excellent way to familiarise yourself with a document's key themes before going deeper.
Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Habits that make your NotebookLM sessions more productive and your outputs more reliable.

📂

One notebook per project or cycle

Keep sources organised by creating dedicated notebooks per grantee, project, or reporting cycle. Mixing sources from different projects in one notebook makes answers less focused and harder to trace back to their origin.

🔍

Always verify citations

Click every citation in NotebookLM's responses to verify the source passage before using the information in a report or formal communication. NotebookLM is highly accurate, but always confirm critical figures and direct quotes.

💬

Ask specific, structured questions

Vague questions get vague answers. Instead of "summarise this report", ask "summarise the biodiversity outcomes reported in section 3 against the targets set in the logframe". The more specific the question, the more useful the output.

📝

Save notes throughout, not at the end

Save useful responses as notes as you work through a review session. Use the Synthesise notes feature at the end to compile them into your review document — much faster than recalling what was covered after the fact.

🔒

Handle sensitive documents with care

Do not upload documents containing sensitive personal data, financial account details, or highly confidential third-party information without checking with your manager first. Standard grantee progress reports and project documents are appropriate for NotebookLM use.

🌐

Use the language feature proactively

When you receive a Spanish or French language report, upload it to NotebookLM and begin your review immediately in English — do not wait for a translation. This can save days on regional programme reviews with multilingual grantees.
