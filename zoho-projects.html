Overview

What is Zoho Projects?

Zoho Projects is CBF’s project and work management tool, part of Zoho One. It turns scattered tasks, deadlines, and updates into one shared, visual plan the whole team can see and move forward together.

More than a to-do list — make it yours

Zoho Projects can run far more than IT work. Anything with steps, owners, and deadlines fits: grant deliverables, events, reports, campaigns, onboarding. This guide covers the essentials, then actively encourages you to get creative and shape it around how your team actually works.

Why It Matters

What it does for you, your team, and CBF

The same tool pays off at three levels. Here is what changes when work lives in Zoho Projects instead of inboxes and spreadsheets.

For you

One clear view of what is on your plate, with due dates and reminders so nothing slips. Log your time, find updates in one place, and stop chasing threads.

For your team

A single shared source of truth. Everyone sees who is doing what, dependencies and the Gantt chart reveal bottlenecks before they bite, and comments keep decisions in context.

For CBF

Visibility across projects and portfolios, consistent templates, and reporting you can hand to funders and management — with the work connected to the rest of Zoho.

The Basics

Setting up and running a project

The core loop is simple: create a project, break the work into tasks, set your key goals as phases, then track and collaborate as the work moves.

2
Create a project and add the work
  1. 1
    Click New Project, give it a name and dates, and start from a template if one fits
  2. 2
    Add Task Lists to group related work (for example, by stage or workstream)
  3. 3
    Add Tasks with an owner, start and due dates, and priority
  4. 4
    Set Phases for the key goals or deadlines the project is built around
Heads up on naming: Milestones are now called Phases in Zoho Projects. If older guides or colleagues say “milestone,” they mean the same thing.
3
Plan, track, and collaborate
  1. 1
    Link related tasks with Dependencies, then open the Gantt chart to see the timeline and spot knock-on delays
  2. 2
    Log effort on the Timesheet as you work
  3. 3
    Raise and resolve problems in the Issues tracker
  4. 4
    @mention colleagues and add Followers so the right people get updates without owning the task
  5. 5
    Switch between List, Kanban, and Gantt views to suit how you think
Get Creative

Creative ways CBF teams can use it

Zoho Projects is not just for IT. The best adoption comes when teams bend it to their own work. A few starting points grounded in what CBF actually does:

Grant deliverable tracking

One project per grant or facility, with phases as reporting milestones and tasks as the deliverables and their due dates.

MEL and indicators

Track indicator data collection, verification, and reporting as task lists and phases, so nothing is missed at reporting time.

Comms content calendar

Plan posts, newsletters, and campaigns as dated tasks with owners, and use the calendar and Gantt views to see the month ahead.

Events and workshops

Build a reusable event template covering logistics, invitations, run-of-show, and follow-up, then clone it for each event.

Board and report prep

Turn recurring board cycles and report deadlines into checklist projects so the same steps happen on time every cycle.

Onboarding and rollouts

Templated projects for new-staff setup or system rollouts give every person the same, complete start.

Tips & Tricks

Get more out of it

Habits and shortcuts that experienced Zoho Projects users rely on.

Start from a template

Build a template once for recurring work, then spin up a ready-structured project in a click instead of starting blank each time.

Phases for goals, lists for grouping

Decide your phases and task lists before piling in tasks; the structure keeps a busy project readable.

Use dependencies

Link tasks that rely on each other so the Gantt chart shows how one slip pushes the rest, before it derails the timeline.

Follow, don’t pester

Add yourself as a follower on a task or phase to get updates automatically, instead of asking for status.

Let Zia draft text

Zoho’s AI assistant, Zia, can generate or rewrite task descriptions, saving time on the wording.

Mobile for updates, desktop for planning

The app is great for quick status changes on the go; do project setup and Gantt work in the browser where it is roomier.

FAQs

Frequently asked questions

The questions new CBF users ask most often.

Can I use it for non-IT work?

Yes. Anything with steps, owners, and deadlines fits — grants, events, comms, reports, onboarding. It is a general work tool, not just a developer tool.

Do external partners need a paid licence?

Partners and vendors can be added as client users with restricted access. Whether that needs a licence depends on CBF’s plan, so check with the System Administrator before inviting outside people.

Can I track my time?

Yes. Log effort against tasks on the Timesheet. It is useful for planning, effort estimates, and funder reporting.

Is there a mobile app?

Yes, for iOS and Android. It is best for updates on the go; some planning features are fuller in the browser, and mobile notifications can lag slightly behind desktop.

Can I import existing plans?

Yes. You can import tasks and start from templates rather than rebuilding by hand. Ask the Administrator if you need help with a bulk import.

What happened to Milestones?

They are now called Phases. Same idea — the key goals or checkpoints a project is built around.

Common Issues

Common issues and how to handle them

Honest, recurring snags other teams report, and the practical fix for each.

“It feels complex and hard to find things”

Fix: The onboarding curve is the most cited hurdle. Start with one small project from a template, live in the My Tasks and Kanban views, and add features as you need them rather than all at once.

“Someone changed or broke my project”

Fix: Use profiles to control who can edit. Give most staff an Employee profile and reserve Manager or Admin for leads, and follow key tasks so you see changes as they happen.

“It gets slow on big projects”

Fix: Performance lags most on very large projects and heavy Gantt use. Split oversized projects into smaller ones or phases, and filter views to what you need.

“Mobile notifications don’t sync instantly”

Fix: Mobile can trail the desktop. When something looks out of date, refresh in the browser for the current state.

“A feature seems to need another Zoho app”

Fix: Some advanced capabilities tie into other Zoho apps or higher plans. Before assuming it is missing, check with the System Administrator about what CBF’s plan includes.

Privacy & Access

Who sees what, and a word on trust

Two fair questions come up with any shared project tool: who can see what, and whether time tracking means being watched. Here is how both work at CBF.

Who can see what

Access is set by a Profile (what you can do) and a Role (where you sit), and it can be tuned per project and per module. External partners are added as Client users who only see what they are given, never the whole portal. Zoho Projects ships with five default profiles:

Admin
Full control of the portal and settings
Manager
Runs projects and manages teams
Employee
Works on assigned projects
Contractor / Client
Limited, restricted access for outside partners
Practical effect: You can customise profiles and restrict modules, so a sensitive project’s data is visible only to the people who need it, and partners see just their slice.

On time tracking and trust

Timesheets and activity logs exist for planning, effort estimates, and funder reporting — not to monitor keystrokes. The goal is fair workload visibility, not surveillance.

The CBF approach: Log time honestly, and managers should use it to balance workload and report to funders, not to police people. If you are ever unsure who can see your activity on a project, ask the System Administrator — access is transparent and adjustable.
