Overview

What is Google Drive?

Google Drive is CBF's active working file system — the place where documents are created, collaborated on, and shared during their lifecycle. It is not the final home for completed work. Once a document is finished and approved, it is archived to Zoho WorkDrive, CBF's official document repository.

Drive's role in CBF's workflow

Think of Google Drive as your active workspace — the place where drafts are written, reports are built, and files are shared with colleagues in real time. It integrates directly with Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, Gmail, and Meet. Once work reaches its final, approved state, it moves to Zoho WorkDrive for long-term, structured storage.

Getting Started

Accessing Google Drive

Drive is available via browser, desktop app, and mobile. All files are stored in the cloud and accessible from any device signed into your CBF Google account.

1
Open Drive on the web
  1. 1
    Go to drive.google.com in any browser, or click the Google apps waffle in Gmail and select Drive
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account. Your My Drive is your personal workspace — files here are private unless you share them
  3. 3
    Shared drives (left sidebar) are team spaces where files belong to CBF, not to any individual — these are used for team and department files
  4. 4
    Shared with me shows files other people have shared with you but that live in their own Drive
Key distinction: Files in My Drive are owned by you — if your account is deactivated, those files may be lost. Files in Shared Drives are owned by CBF and remain accessible to the team regardless of staff changes. Always store team files in Shared Drives.
2
Install the Drive desktop app
  1. 1
    Download Google Drive for Desktop from drive.google.com/drive/apps
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account — Drive appears as a folder in your file explorer (Windows) or Finder (Mac)
  3. 3
    Files sync automatically between your computer and the cloud — changes made offline sync when you reconnect to the internet
Useful for: Working on files without a browser open, accessing Drive files from desktop applications, and ensuring you always have the latest version of shared files without manually downloading them.
File Organisation

Organising your files and folders

A well-organised Drive makes it easy for the whole team to find what they need. Follow CBF's naming and folder conventions consistently.

3
Create and name folders
  1. 1
    Click + New (top-left) → New folder. Give the folder a clear, descriptive name
  2. 2
    Use a consistent naming format — include the year, project name, or department where relevant. Example: 2026_ReefResilience_Reports or Finance_2026_Q1
  3. 3
    To move a file into a folder: right-click the file → Move to → select the destination folder
  4. 4
    To colour-code a folder: right-click it → Change colour — use colours to distinguish project folders, teams, or priority levels at a glance
CBF naming conventions: Use underscores instead of spaces, include the year at the start of folder names, and avoid vague names like "Misc" or "New folder". Consistent naming makes search and handovers much easier.
4
Search and filter files
  1. 1
    Use the search bar at the top of Drive — search by file name, content within files, or the name of a person who shared the file
  2. 2
    Click the search filter icon (right side of the search bar) to filter by file type, owner, modification date, or location
  3. 3
    Use Recent in the left sidebar to quickly find files you have opened or edited recently
  4. 4
    Star important files: right-click → Add to Starred. Starred files appear in the Starred section for fast access
Core Tasks

Uploading and creating files

Files can be uploaded from your computer or created directly in Drive using Google Docs, Sheets, or Slides. Creating files natively in Google format is preferred for collaboration.

5
Upload files and folders
  1. 1
    Click + New → File upload or Folder upload to upload from your computer
  2. 2
    Alternatively, drag and drop files directly into the Drive browser window — they upload automatically to the folder you are currently viewing
  3. 3
    Uploaded Word, Excel, and PowerPoint files can be converted to Google format — right-click the file → Open with → select the relevant Google app. This enables real-time collaboration
File format tip: When working on documents with external partners who use Microsoft Office, keep files in their original format (.docx, .xlsx, .pptx). For internal CBF work, use Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides to take full advantage of collaboration features.
6
Create new Google files directly in Drive
  1. 1
    Click + New → choose Google Docs, Google Sheets, or Google Slides to create a new file
  2. 2
    The file opens immediately in a new browser tab and is saved automatically in real time — there is no "Save" button needed
  3. 3
    Rename the file by clicking Untitled document at the top and typing your chosen name
  4. 4
    The file is created in the folder you were viewing in Drive — check the location shown at the top of the document and move it if needed
Auto-save: Google files save every few seconds automatically. There is no risk of losing work due to a missed save — but always ensure you have internet connectivity when working on shared files.
Sharing Files

Sharing files and folders

Sharing is one of Drive's most powerful features — and one that requires the most care. Always share with the minimum level of access needed.

7
Share a file with specific people
  1. 1
    Right-click a file → Share → Share again to open the sharing panel (or open the file and click the Share button top-right)
  2. 2
    In the Add people and groups field, type the person's name or CBF email address
  3. 3
    Select the appropriate permission level from the dropdown — Viewer, Commenter, or Editor (see Permissions section below)
  4. 4
    Add an optional message explaining why you are sharing, then click Send
Before sharing externally: Check with your manager before sharing CBF files with anyone outside the organisation. Never share files containing financial data, personal information, or confidential project details with external parties without authorisation.
8
Share via link
  1. 1
    Open the sharing panel → click Change under General access (or Copy link)
  2. 2
    Choose who can access the link:
    — Restricted: only people explicitly added can open it (recommended for most CBF files)
    — Anyone with the link: anyone who receives the link can open it (use only for public resources)
  3. 3
    Set the permission level for link access (Viewer, Commenter, or Editor), then click Copy link and paste it where needed
Default to Restricted: Always use Restricted link sharing for internal CBF documents. "Anyone with the link" means anyone on the internet — only use this for intentionally public content such as published reports or outreach materials.
9
Remove or change sharing access
  1. 1
    Open the sharing panel for the file → find the person whose access you want to change
  2. 2
    Click the permission dropdown next to their name → select a new level or click Remove access
  3. 3
    Click Save — the person is notified if you choose to send a notification, but not by default
Good practice: Periodically review who has access to your shared files — especially for sensitive documents. Remove access for colleagues who have moved to a different project or left CBF.
Access Control

Understanding permissions

Google Drive has five permission levels. Choosing the right one protects CBF's files while enabling effective collaboration.

Role What they can do When to use at CBF
Viewer Read the file only. Cannot edit, comment, or download (unless download is enabled) Sharing final documents for reference. Sharing reports with external stakeholders
Commenter Add comments and suggestions but cannot directly edit the content Sending a document for review or feedback without allowing direct edits
Editor View, edit, comment, and share the file. Can also change sharing settings Active collaborators working on a document together. Use within the CBF team
Organiser In Shared Drives — can add/remove members and reorganise the drive structure Team leads or project managers responsible for a Shared Drive
Manager Full control of a Shared Drive — can delete the drive, manage all members and content System Administrator or senior staff only. Assigned by IT
Principle of least privilege: Always grant the minimum level of access needed. If someone only needs to read a document, give them Viewer access — not Editor. This reduces the risk of accidental changes or data exposure.
Team Spaces

Shared Drives

Shared Drives are team-owned spaces within Google Drive. Unlike My Drive, files here belong to CBF — not to individual staff members. All team and project files should live here.

10
Access and use a Shared Drive
  1. 1
    In the left sidebar of Drive, click Shared drives to see all Shared Drives you have been given access to
  2. 2
    Navigate into the relevant Shared Drive and follow the folder structure established by your team or manager
  3. 3
    Create files and folders within the Shared Drive the same way as in My Drive — the key difference is that ownership belongs to CBF, not you personally
  4. 4
    If you cannot find a Shared Drive you need access to, contact your manager or the System Administrator
Why Shared Drives matter: If a staff member leaves CBF and files are only in their My Drive, those files may become inaccessible. Keeping team files in Shared Drives ensures continuity regardless of staff changes.
11
Version history — recover earlier versions of a file
  1. 1
    Open the file → click File in the top menu → Version history → See version history
  2. 2
    A panel appears on the right showing all saved versions with timestamps and the name of who made each change
  3. 3
    Click any version to preview it — then click Restore this version to roll back to that state
Never lose work: Google Drive keeps version history automatically for all Google files. If a colleague accidentally deletes content or overwrites something important, version history lets you recover it without contacting IT.
CBF File Lifecycle

Google Drive vs Zoho WorkDrive

Understanding when to use each platform is essential for keeping CBF's records organised and accessible long-term. The two platforms serve different stages of a document's life.

12
The CBF document lifecycle
1
Create & Collaborate — Google Drive
Draft the document in Google Docs, Sheets, or Slides. Share with colleagues for input and review using comments and suggestions. Iterate until the content is ready for approval.
2
Review & Approve — Google Drive
Share the document for formal review via comment access. Address feedback, apply final edits, and gain sign-off from the relevant manager or team lead. The document is now finalised.
3
Archive — Zoho WorkDrive
Export the final document (PDF or original format as appropriate) and upload it to the correct folder in Zoho WorkDrive. This is CBF's permanent, structured record. The file in Google Drive may be retained for reference but WorkDrive is the authoritative copy.
📁
Zoho WorkDrive is the system of record at CBF When a document is complete and approved, it belongs in WorkDrive — not just in Google Drive. This ensures CBF's institutional knowledge is preserved, organised, and accessible to all authorised staff, even as team members change over time. See the Zoho WorkDrive training guide for full instructions on how to upload and organise files there.
Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Habits that keep Google Drive clean, collaborative, and useful for the whole team.

Store team files in Shared Drives

Never keep team documents in My Drive only. Shared Drives are owned by CBF — files remain accessible even if a staff member's account changes.

Use Suggested Edits, not tracked changes

In Google Docs, switch to Suggesting mode before editing a shared document. Changes appear as suggestions that the owner can accept or reject — preserving the original content.

Name files clearly from the start

Rename files before sharing them. "Copy of Untitled" or "Document 3" wastes everyone's time when searching. Include the date, topic, and version in the file name.

Review sharing settings regularly

Right-click a file → Manage access to see who has access. Remove people who no longer need it. This is especially important for files containing financial or personal data.

Archive to WorkDrive on completion

Once a document is final and approved, upload it to Zoho WorkDrive promptly. Do not leave finalised documents sitting only in Google Drive where they may be accidentally edited.

Use shortcut links, not copies

Instead of duplicating a file into multiple folders, create a shortcut: right-click a file → Add shortcut to Drive. The file lives in one place, accessible from many locations.
