Overview

What is Google Meet?

Google Meet is CBF's primary video conferencing tool. It is built into Google Calendar and Gmail, making it the default platform for all internal meetings, partner calls, and virtual events.

How CBF uses Google Meet

Every meeting created in Google Calendar automatically includes a Meet link. CBF staff use Meet for internal check-ins, project team meetings, partner and donor calls, board meetings, and all-staff sessions. No separate app installation is required — Meet runs entirely in your browser, though a mobile app is also available.

Platform
Google Meet (Workspace)
Access
Browser (no install needed) + mobile app
Capacity
Up to 500 participants (Workspace)
Integration
Auto-linked in Calendar & Gmail invites
Getting Started

Joining a meeting

There are several ways to join a Google Meet — from your calendar, a Gmail invite, a direct link, or a meeting code. All routes lead to the same place.

1
Join from Google Calendar
  1. 1
    Open Google Calendar and click on the meeting event
  2. 2
    Click the Join with Google Meet button in the event pop-up — this opens Meet directly in your browser
  3. 3
    On the preview screen, check your camera and microphone settings before joining. Toggle them on or off as needed
  4. 4
    Click Join now to enter the meeting
Tip: The preview screen shows how you appear to others before you join. Take a moment to check your background, lighting, and audio before entering.
2
Join from Gmail or a meeting link
  1. 1
    From Gmail: Open the meeting invitation email and click the Join with Google Meet button or the Meet link in the email body
  2. 2
    From a shared link: Click the link in any message or document — it opens Meet in your browser automatically
  3. 3
    From meet.google.com: Go to meet.google.com, click Enter a code or link, and paste the meeting code (format: abc-defg-hij)
External guests joining without a Google account will be placed in a waiting room. As the meeting host or a CBF participant, you will see a notification to admit them — always admit known participants promptly.
3
Join on mobile
  1. 1
    Download the Google Meet app from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account
  3. 3
    Upcoming meetings from your Calendar appear on the home screen — tap Join to enter, or tap the Meet link in your Calendar or Gmail app
For important meetings: Whenever possible, join from a laptop or desktop for the best experience — better screen real estate, easier screen sharing, and more stable audio. Use mobile as a backup when needed.
In-Meeting Controls

Meeting controls quick reference

All main controls appear in the toolbar at the bottom of the Meet screen. Here is what each does.

Microphone
Mute / unmute. Keyboard shortcut: Ctrl+D (PC) or ⌘+D (Mac)
Camera
Turn video on / off. Shortcut: Ctrl+E or ⌘+E
Present
Share your screen, a window, or a tab with participants
Chat
Send text messages to all participants during the meeting
People
View participant list, admit waiting guests, mute others (host)
More options
Access recording, noise cancellation, captions, layouts, and settings
Raise hand
Signal you want to speak without interrupting — the host sees a notification
Leave call
Exit the meeting. Hosts: use "End call for all" to close the meeting for everyone
Host Controls

Hosting a meeting

As the meeting organiser or host at CBF, you have additional controls to manage the room, admit guests, and keep the session running smoothly.

4
Start and manage your meeting
  1. 1
    Join the meeting a few minutes early to set up — check audio, video, and any documents or slides you plan to share
  2. 2
    Click the People icon to see who has joined and admit any participants waiting in the lobby
  3. 3
    To mute a disruptive participant: click their name in the People panel → click the microphone icon next to their name. Note: you cannot unmute others — only they can unmute themselves
  4. 4
    To remove a participant: click their name → click the three-dot menu → Remove from meeting. Use this sparingly and only when necessary
  5. 5
    When the meeting is over, click the red Leave call button → select End call for everyone to close the session for all participants
Host tip: Arrive 5 minutes early for external or partner meetings. This gives you time to test your setup and admit guests promptly as they arrive — it reflects well on CBF's professionalism.
5
Manage host controls & meeting security
  1. 1
    Click the shield / lock icon at the bottom of the screen (or via More options → Host controls) to access security settings
  2. 2
    Toggle Quick access off for sensitive meetings — this places all new joiners in the waiting room for manual approval, even those with the link
  3. 3
    Toggle Share their screen off to prevent participants from screen sharing without permission — useful for large all-staff or partner presentations
  4. 4
    Toggle Send chat messages off to disable the in-meeting chat if it is becoming a distraction during formal sessions
For board meetings or confidential sessions: Always disable Quick Access and enable the waiting room. Only admit participants you recognise. Do not share Meet links in public channels or on social media.
6
Use breakout rooms
  1. 1
    Click Activities (grid icon in the bottom toolbar) → select Breakout rooms
  2. 2
    Set the number of rooms and choose to assign participants manually or let Meet assign them randomly
  3. 3
    Click Open rooms — participants receive a notification to join their assigned room
  4. 4
    To bring everyone back: click Close all rooms — a 60-second warning is sent to all breakout rooms before they are closed
When to use breakout rooms: Workshops, training sessions, or large team meetings where smaller group discussion is needed. Works well for up to 100 participants split across up to 100 rooms.
Presenting

Sharing your screen

Screen sharing in Google Meet lets you present documents, slides, reports, or any content to all participants in real time.

7
Share your screen, a window, or a tab
  1. 1
    Click the Present now button (screen icon) in the bottom toolbar
  2. 2
    Choose what to share:
    — Your entire screen: shows everything on your display
    — A window: shares only one specific application (recommended)
    — A tab: shares a single browser tab, with audio included (best for videos)
  3. 3
    Select your choice and click Share. A red border appears around whatever is being shared
  4. 4
    To stop sharing: click Stop presenting in the Meet toolbar or in the notification bar at the bottom of your screen
Best practice — share a window, not your whole screen: Sharing a specific window prevents participants from accidentally seeing notifications, personal files, or other open tabs. Close unnecessary tabs and turn off notifications before presenting.
8
Present Google Slides directly in Meet
  1. 1
    Open your Google Slides presentation in a browser tab before the meeting
  2. 2
    In Meet, click Present now → A Tab → select the Slides tab
  3. 3
    Alternatively, in Google Slides click Slideshow → Present to a meeting — this connects directly to your active Meet session
  4. 4
    Use the arrow keys or on-screen controls to advance slides — participants see each slide in real time
Smooth presentations: Open your slides and run through them once before the meeting starts. Check that all embedded links, videos, and images load correctly before sharing your screen.
Recording

Recording meetings

Google Meet allows meetings to be recorded and saved to Google Drive. Recordings should only be made with participant consent.

9
Record a meeting
  1. 1
    Click the three-dot menu (More options) in the bottom toolbar → select Record meeting
  2. 2
    A notification is sent to all participants that the meeting is being recorded. A red recording indicator appears on screen
  3. 3
    To stop recording: click the three-dot menu again → Stop recording. Recording also stops automatically when everyone leaves the meeting
  4. 4
    The recording is saved to the host's Google Drive under Meet Recordings, and a link is automatically sent to the host via Gmail. Share the Drive link with participants as needed
Consent is required: Always inform and gain agreement from all participants before recording — especially for meetings with external guests, partners, or donors. Do not record without prior notice. This is both a professional and legal obligation.
Storing recordings: Move recording files from your personal Drive to the relevant shared CBF project folder in Google Drive promptly after the meeting, so the team can access them.
Collaboration Tools

In-meeting chat, captions & Q&A

Google Meet includes several built-in tools that improve communication and accessibility during meetings.

10
Use the in-meeting chat
  1. 1
    Click the Chat bubble icon in the toolbar to open the chat panel on the right side of the screen
  2. 2
    Type your message and press Enter to send — all participants can see the chat
  3. 3
    Use chat to share links, resources, or questions without interrupting the speaker
Note: Chat messages are visible to all participants but are not saved after the meeting ends unless the meeting is recorded. Copy any important links or action items from chat before leaving.
11
Turn on live captions
  1. 1
    Click the three-dot menu (More options) → select Turn on captions
  2. 2
    Real-time captions generated from speech appear at the bottom of the screen — captions are visible only to you unless all participants enable them individually
  3. 3
    To turn off: return to More options → Turn off captions
Accessibility at CBF: Encourage all participants to turn on captions when meetings include colleagues or partners for whom English is a second language, or in noisy environments. It improves comprehension for everyone.
12
Use the Q&A feature (for large meetings)
  1. 1
    Click Activities (grid icon) → select Q&A
  2. 2
    Participants can submit questions via the Q&A panel — questions can be upvoted by others so the most relevant ones rise to the top
  3. 3
    The host or a moderator works through questions, marking them as answered
When to use Q&A: Best for all-staff meetings, partner webinars, or any session with 15+ participants where open chat would become overwhelming. For smaller meetings, the raise hand function is simpler.
CBF Meeting Culture

Video meeting etiquette at CBF

Professional conduct in virtual meetings reflects CBF's values — especially when partners, donors, or community members are present.

Do
✓Join on time — log in 2 minutes early for internal meetings, 5 minutes for external ones
✓Mute yourself when not speaking to reduce background noise
✓Use a neutral, professional background — physical or a clean virtual background
✓Turn your camera on when possible — it improves engagement and connection
✓Use raise hand to signal you want to speak rather than talking over others
✓Inform the host in advance if you need to leave early
Don't
✕Join from a noisy or public environment without muting — use headphones if necessary
✕Multitask visibly — avoid typing loudly or looking away repeatedly during discussions
✕Share confidential meeting content, links, or recordings without authorisation
✕Admit unknown participants from the waiting room without verifying their identity
✕Record a meeting without informing all participants first
✕Leave a meeting without saying goodbye in the chat — especially in small meetings
Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Small habits that make your virtual meetings more productive and professional.

Use noise cancellation

Go to More options → Settings → Audio → enable Noise cancellation. This filters out background sounds like fans, typing, and ambient noise automatically.

Pin important speakers

Right-click a participant's video tile and select Pin to keep their video on screen regardless of who is speaking. Useful for keeping the presenter always visible.

Change your layout

Go to More options → Change layout to switch between Tiled (see everyone), Spotlight (focus on presenter), or Sidebar. Choose what suits the meeting format.

Take notes in Google Docs

Open a shared Google Doc during the meeting for live note-taking. Assign someone to capture action items, decisions, and owners — share the doc with all participants after.

Test before important calls

Before partner or donor meetings, do a quick audio and video test. Go to meet.google.com and click Start an instant meeting — check your setup alone before your guests arrive.

Use keyboard shortcuts

Speed up common actions — Ctrl+D / ⌘+D to mute/unmute, Ctrl+E / ⌘+E to toggle camera, and press ? inside Meet to see all available shortcuts.
