Overview

What is Google Calendar?

Google Calendar is CBF's official scheduling platform. It keeps individual and team schedules visible and coordinated — from internal meetings and project deadlines to partner calls and organisation-wide events.

How CBF uses Google Calendar

Every CBF staff member has a personal calendar connected to their CBF Google account. Shared team and organisation-wide calendars layer on top, giving everyone visibility into deadlines, meetings, and key events. Calendar is tightly integrated with Gmail and Google Meet — meeting invites, video links, and reminders all flow through a single system.

Types of calendars at CBF

Personal calendar

Your individual CBF calendar. Visible only to you unless you choose to share it.

Team calendars

Shared within departments or project teams. Used for team meetings, deadlines, and milestones.

Organisation calendar

CBF-wide events — all-staff meetings, public holidays, institutional deadlines. Managed by administration.

Other people's calendars

You can view colleague's free/busy status to schedule without conflicts. Access depends on their sharing settings.

Getting Started

Accessing Google Calendar

Calendar is available via browser, mobile app, and directly from Gmail. Use your CBF Google account to sign in.

1
Open Google Calendar on the web
  1. 1
    Go to calendar.google.com in any browser — or click the grid icon (Google apps waffle) in Gmail and select Calendar
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account if prompted
  3. 3
    Your personal calendar loads by default. Shared CBF calendars you have access to will appear in the Other calendars section in the left sidebar
2
Set up the Google Calendar mobile app
  1. 1
    Download Google Calendar from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account — all your calendars and shared calendars sync automatically
  3. 3
    Tap the three-line menu to toggle which calendars are visible on your mobile view
Tip: Enable notifications on mobile so you receive meeting reminders even when away from your desk. Go to the app Settings → tap your account → Notifications to customise.
Core Tasks

Creating events

Events are the building block of Google Calendar. Whether it's a quick internal check-in or a multi-day partner workshop, the process is the same.

3
Create a new event
  1. 1
    Click + Create (top-left) or click directly on any time slot in the calendar grid to open a new event
  2. 2
    Enter a clear, descriptive title — include the topic and any key context (e.g. CBF Q3 Budget Review – Finance Team)
  3. 3
    Set the date and time. For recurring events, click Does not repeat and choose the frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, custom)
  4. 4
    Add a location — either a physical address or a Google Meet link (see Scheduling Tools section). For hybrid meetings, include both
  5. 5
    Add a description — include the agenda, any prep required, and links to relevant documents in Google Drive
  6. 6
    Click Save to add the event to your calendar, or More options to access advanced settings before saving
Event titles matter: Other CBF staff who can see your calendar will only see the event title. Make it informative enough to avoid confusion — avoid vague titles like "Meeting" or "Call".
4
Create an all-day or multi-day event
  1. 1
    Click on the date at the top of the calendar grid (the date header area, not a time slot) — this opens a new all-day event by default
  2. 2
    Alternatively, click + Create → toggle on All day in the event form
  3. 3
    For multi-day events (e.g. a workshop, conference, or leave period), set the end date by clicking the end date field and selecting the last day
For annual leave: Create an all-day event on your personal calendar for the duration of your leave. Mark yourself as Out of office using the event type selector — Google will automatically decline meetings sent to you during this period.
Core Tasks

Inviting others & managing RSVPs

Google Calendar makes it easy to invite colleagues and external partners, check availability before scheduling, and track who has accepted.

5
Add guests to an event
  1. 1
    In the event creation form, click the Add guests field and start typing a name or email address — CBF contacts appear as suggestions from the directory
  2. 2
    For external guests (partners, donors, consultants), type their full email address
  3. 3
    Click Guest permissions to control whether guests can: modify the event, invite others, or see the guest list. Adjust as needed
  4. 4
    Save the event — each guest automatically receives an email invitation with the event details and an Accept / Decline / Maybe option
Tracking RSVPs: Open the event and look at the guest list — you will see a green tick (accepted), red X (declined), or question mark (awaiting response) next to each guest's name.
6
Check availability before scheduling
  1. 1
    When adding guests to an event, click View schedules (or the grid icon next to the guest list) — this opens the Find a time view
  2. 2
    Each guest's calendar appears as a colour band. White gaps indicate everyone is free — click a gap to set the event time
  3. 3
    Alternatively, use the Suggested times feature — Calendar will automatically propose slots when all guests are available
Best practice: Always check availability before sending an invite rather than sending and hoping. This shows respect for colleagues' time and reduces the back-and-forth of rescheduling.
7
Respond to a meeting invitation
  1. 1
    Meeting invitations arrive in your Gmail inbox with Accept / Maybe / Decline buttons — click one directly from the email
  2. 2
    You can also respond directly from Google Calendar — click the event on your calendar and select your response from the RSVP options
  3. 3
    If declining or marking as Maybe, add a brief note to the organiser explaining why — this is good professional practice at CBF
  4. 4
    To propose a new time: click More options in the invitation → select Propose a new time and suggest an alternative slot
Shared Calendars

Shared & team calendars

CBF uses shared calendars to keep teams aligned. Understanding how to subscribe to and use these calendars is essential for staying informed about team-wide and organisation-wide events.

8
Subscribe to a shared CBF calendar
  1. 1
    In the left sidebar under Other calendars, click the + icon → select Subscribe to calendar
  2. 2
    Type the name or email address of the shared calendar (your manager or IT will provide the calendar address)
  3. 3
    Click the calendar name once subscribed to customise its display colour — use a colour that helps distinguish it from your personal calendar
New starters: Your manager will share the relevant team and organisation calendars with you during onboarding. If you are missing a calendar you need, contact your manager or the System Administrator.
9
Share your own calendar with a colleague
Settings ⚙→ Settings for my calendars→ Select your calendar→ Share with specific people
  1. 1
    Click + Add people and enter the colleague's CBF email address
  2. 2
    Choose the permission level: See only free/busy (recommended for most colleagues), See all event details, or Make changes to events
  3. 3
    Click Send — the colleague will receive an email to add your calendar to their view
CBF default: Sharing free/busy status with all CBF colleagues makes scheduling significantly easier across the organisation. Consider enabling this as your default sharing setting.
Scheduling Tools

Scheduling tools & Google Meet

Google Calendar integrates directly with Google Meet to generate video conference links automatically, and includes tools to streamline how meetings are booked.

10
Add a Google Meet link to an event
  1. 1
    In the event creation form, click Add Google Meet video conferencing — a unique Meet link is generated automatically and added to the event
  2. 2
    The Meet link appears in the event details and in every guest's calendar invitation — no separate link sharing is needed
  3. 3
    To join a meeting: click the event in your calendar and click Join with Google Meet — or open the link directly from your Gmail invitation
Standard practice at CBF: Add a Google Meet link to all internal meetings, even if the meeting may be held in person. This ensures remote or travelling colleagues can always join.
11
Use appointment scheduling (for external bookings)
  1. 1
    Click + Create → select Appointment schedule from the event type options
  2. 2
    Set your availability window and slot duration (e.g. 30-minute slots, Monday–Friday 9am–12pm)
  3. 3
    A booking page link is generated — share this with external partners or consultants so they can self-schedule without email back-and-forth
  4. 4
    When someone books a slot, it automatically blocks your calendar and sends both parties a confirmation with a Meet link
When to use this: Ideal for consultation slots, onboarding calls with new partners, or any scenario where external stakeholders need to book time with a CBF staff member.
Personal Settings

Setting working hours & time zone

CBF operates across multiple Caribbean time zones. Setting your working hours and time zone accurately helps colleagues schedule meetings at appropriate times.

12
Set your working hours
Settings ⚙→ General→ Working hours & location
  1. 1
    Toggle on Enable working hours and set your start and end times for each working day
  2. 2
    Uncheck any non-working days (e.g. Saturday and Sunday)
  3. 3
    Click Save — colleagues will now see a notification if they try to schedule a meeting outside your working hours
13
Set your primary time zone
Settings ⚙→ General→ Time zone
  1. 1
    Under Primary time zone, select your local time zone (e.g. America/Jamaica for AST)
  2. 2
    If you frequently work with colleagues in a different time zone, enable Display secondary time zone and add their zone — both times show on your calendar grid
  3. 3
    Click Save
Important for CBF: Always confirm which time zone is shown when scheduling meetings that include colleagues from different Caribbean territories. Specify the time zone explicitly in the event description when inviting across regions.
CBF Calendar Culture

Calendar etiquette at CBF

How you manage your calendar reflects your professionalism and consideration for your colleagues. These expectations apply to all CBF staff.

Do
✓Keep your calendar up to date so colleagues can see your accurate availability
✓Include a clear agenda or description in every meeting invite
✓Respond to invitations promptly — accept, decline, or propose a new time
✓Block focus time on your calendar so colleagues know when you are unavailable for meetings
✓Set your Out of Office status on Calendar when on leave — this auto-declines new invites
Don't
✕Schedule meetings without checking guests' availability first
✕Send invites with no description or agenda — especially for external meetings
✕Leave invitations without a response — unanswered invites create uncertainty for organisers
✕Schedule meetings during colleagues' marked-out focus or personal time without asking first
✕Send last-minute meeting invites (under 24 hours notice) except in genuine emergencies
Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Small habits that keep your calendar — and your team's — running smoothly.

Use colour coding

Assign different colours to different calendars or event types (e.g. green for project meetings, blue for partner calls). This makes your week readable at a glance.

Block focus time

Create "Focus Time" events on your calendar for deep work. Mark them as Busy so colleagues know not to schedule meetings during those slots.

Add documents to events

Attach relevant Google Drive files directly to the meeting invite. Guests can review materials before the meeting without hunting for links in email threads.

Set smart reminders

Add a 10-minute reminder for internal meetings and a 30-minute reminder for external calls — gives you time to prepare without being overwhelmed by alerts.

End meetings 5 minutes early

When creating recurring meetings, set them to end 5 minutes before the hour (e.g. 9:00–9:55 AM). This gives everyone transition time between back-to-back meetings.

Search past events

Use the search bar in Google Calendar to find past meetings, notes, and attendees — useful for locating recurring event details or confirming when something was last discussed.
