Overview

What is Gemini?

Gemini is Google's AI assistant — available as a standalone tool at gemini.google.com and built directly into Google Workspace apps including Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Drive. It helps CBF staff write faster, think through problems, summarise content, and automate repetitive tasks using natural language instructions.

How Gemini fits into CBF's work

Gemini is a general-purpose AI assistant that draws on broad world knowledge to help with writing, research, analysis, and ideation. Unlike NotebookLM — which is anchored strictly to documents you upload — Gemini can help with tasks that don't require a specific source, like drafting a new email, brainstorming project ideas, explaining a concept, or rewriting a paragraph. Within Google Workspace, Gemini acts as an intelligent writing and thinking partner embedded in the tools you already use every day.

Getting Started

Accessing Gemini

Gemini is available in two ways — as a standalone assistant at gemini.google.com and as an embedded feature within the Google Workspace apps you already use.

1
Access Gemini as a standalone assistant
  1. 1
    Go to gemini.google.com in any browser
  2. 2
    Sign in with your CBF Google account — use your firstnameinitiallastname@caribbeanbiodiversityfund.org address
  3. 3
    The Gemini interface opens with a conversation window — type your request in plain language and press Enter
  4. 4
    Previous conversations are saved in the left sidebar — click any conversation to continue it or start a fresh one
Sign in with your CBF account: Using your CBF Google account gives Gemini access to your Google Workspace context — it can reference your Drive files and integrate with Gmail and Calendar when needed. Always use your CBF account for work-related Gemini sessions.
2
Access Gemini inside Google Workspace apps
  1. 1
    In Gmail: Click the Gemini star icon (✦) in the compose window or in the right sidebar to open Gemini — use it to draft, refine, or summarise emails
  2. 2
    In Google Docs: Click Ask Gemini (✦ icon, top right) or click the Gemini icon in the left margin next to any paragraph — use it to draft sections, improve text, or generate content
  3. 3
    In Google Sheets: Click the Gemini icon in the toolbar — use it to generate formulas, analyse data, or create charts from natural language descriptions
  4. 4
    In Google Slides: Click Ask Gemini in the toolbar — use it to generate slide content, rewrite text, or create speaker notes from bullet points
Gemini is always where you are: The most efficient way to use Gemini is directly inside the app you are already working in. You do not need to switch tabs — the Gemini panel opens alongside your document, email, or spreadsheet without interrupting your flow.
Knowing Which Tool to Use

Gemini vs NotebookLM — when to use which

Both are AI tools available to all CBF staff through your Google account. The key difference is what they draw on when answering. Understanding this distinction helps you choose the right tool for every task.

Use Gemini when
Gemini
Writing or drafting from scratch — emails, reports, proposals, agendas
Brainstorming ideas, frameworks, or approaches
Explaining concepts or topics you want to understand better
Improving or rewriting existing text
Working inside Gmail, Docs, Sheets, or Slides
Tasks that benefit from broad general knowledge
Use NotebookLM when
NotebookLM
Reviewing specific CBF documents — grantee reports, board papers, project files
Asking questions that require answers grounded in specific source documents
Comparing or synthesising multiple documents
You need cited, traceable answers from known sources
Working with documents in multiple languages

The simplest way to decide: If you are working with specific CBF documents and need accurate, cited answers from them — use NotebookLM. If you need help writing, thinking, or working within a Google app — use Gemini. Many workflows use both: Gemini to draft, NotebookLM to verify from sources.
Getting the Best Results

How to write effective prompts

The quality of what Gemini produces depends almost entirely on how clearly you describe what you need. These four principles will immediately improve your results.

01
Give context
Tell Gemini who you are, what CBF does, and what the output is for. Context shapes the tone, depth, and relevance of the response.
Instead of: "Write an email" — try: "Write a professional email from a Caribbean conservation fund to a grantee requesting their Q2 progress report"
02
Specify the format
Tell Gemini exactly what you want back — a bullet list, a 3-paragraph summary, a table, a draft email, a 5-point agenda. Vague requests get generic outputs.
Instead of: "Summarise this" — try: "Summarise this in 4 bullet points, each under 20 words, suitable for a slide presentation"
03
Set the tone
Specify whether the output should be formal, professional, concise, technical, or accessible. CBF communications typically require a professional but approachable tone.
Add: "Write in a professional, concise tone suitable for an international conservation organisation"
04
Iterate and refine
Gemini remembers your conversation. If the first response is not quite right, follow up: ask it to make it shorter, more formal, or to add a specific point. You rarely need to start over.
Follow up with: "Make this more concise" or "Add a paragraph about CBF's Caribbean mandate" or "Rewrite the opening line"
3
CBF-specific prompt examples

These prompts are tailored to CBF's day-to-day work. Adapt them with the specific details of your task:

Partner email
Draft a professional email from CBF to a partner organisation inviting them to a virtual project review meeting. Tone: formal but warm. Include a request to confirm availability within 5 business days.
Meeting agenda
Create a 60-minute meeting agenda for a CBF project review covering: progress update, budget review, risks and mitigation, next steps, and AOB. Include suggested time allocations.
Report section
Write a 2-paragraph introduction for a Caribbean biodiversity conservation programme report. Highlight the importance of conservation finance for small island developing states. Professional tone.
Slide content
Generate 5 bullet points for a slide titled "CBF's Impact in 2026" covering: countries supported, funds disbursed, species protected, communities engaged, and key partnerships. Concise, presentation-ready language.
Concept explanation
Explain ecosystem-based adaptation in plain language suitable for a non-technical audience. Keep it under 150 words and include one Caribbean example.
Text improvement
Rewrite the following paragraph to be more concise and professional, suitable for a donor report: [paste your text here]. Keep the key facts but reduce the length by 30%.
Core Tasks

Key tasks for CBF staff

These are the most practical, time-saving Gemini tasks for CBF's daily work — ready to use immediately.

4
Draft and improve emails
  1. 1
    In Gmail, open a new compose window → click the Gemini star icon ✦ → select Help me write
  2. 2
    Describe what you need in plain language — for example: "Write a follow-up email to a grantee who has not submitted their Q1 report. Professional but firm tone."
  3. 3
    Gemini generates a draft — review it, click Insert to add it to your compose window, then edit as needed before sending
  4. 4
    To improve an email you have already written: select the text → click the Gemini icon → choose Refine options such as Formalise, Shorten, or Elaborate
Always review before sending: Gemini drafts are a starting point, not a finished product. Always read the full draft, personalise it with specific names and details, and confirm the tone is appropriate for the recipient before sending any CBF communication.
5
Draft and improve documents in Google Docs
  1. 1
    Open a Google Doc → click the Gemini ✦ icon in the top right or click Insert → Help me write to open the Gemini panel
  2. 2
    Type your request — for example: "Write a 3-paragraph executive summary for a project progress report on coral reef restoration in the Eastern Caribbean"
  3. 3
    Gemini generates the content — click Insert to add it to your document at the cursor position
  4. 4
    To improve existing text: select a paragraph → click the Gemini icon that appears → choose from Shorten, Elaborate, Rephrase, or type a custom instruction
Use Gemini to break writer's block: When starting a new report or proposal feels daunting, ask Gemini to generate a rough first draft or an outline. Even an imperfect draft gives you something concrete to edit — which is always faster than writing from a blank page.
6
Work with data in Google Sheets
  1. 1
    Open a Google Sheet → click the Gemini icon in the toolbar to open the Gemini panel
  2. 2
    Ask Gemini to create a formula in plain language — for example: "Write a formula to sum all values in column D where column B says 'Active'" — Gemini writes the SUMIF formula and explains it
  3. 3
    Ask Gemini to analyse your data — for example: "What are the top 3 spending categories in this sheet?" — Gemini reads the visible data and provides a plain-language summary
  4. 4
    Ask Gemini to create a table or tracker from scratch — for example: "Create a project activity tracker with columns for activity name, responsible person, start date, due date, status, and notes"
7
Generate slide content in Google Slides
  1. 1
    Open a Google Slides presentation → click Ask Gemini ✦ in the toolbar
  2. 2
    Ask Gemini to generate content for a specific slide — for example: "Write concise bullet points for a slide titled 'Why Caribbean Biodiversity Matters' — 4 points, each under 12 words"
  3. 3
    Ask Gemini to generate speaker notes from your slide bullets — select the text on a slide and ask: "Write 3 sentences of speaker notes expanding on these bullet points"
  4. 4
    Ask Gemini to suggest a slide structure for a presentation — for example: "Suggest a 10-slide structure for a donor briefing on CBF's reef resilience programme"
8
Summarise emails and threads in Gmail
  1. 1
    Open a long email thread in Gmail → click the Summarise this email button (Gemini ✦ icon at the top of the thread)
  2. 2
    Gemini generates a concise summary of the thread — the key points, decisions made, and any action items identified
  3. 3
    This is particularly useful for catching up on long project threads after leave, or before jumping into a conversation that has been running for several days
Useful for onboarding: New CBF staff can use Gemini's email summarisation to quickly get up to speed on active project threads they have been added to — without reading every message in a long chain.

CBF Policies

Responsible use of Gemini at CBF

Gemini is a powerful tool — and like all AI tools, it requires professional judgement. These rules protect CBF, its partners, and its data.

Non-negotiable rules
✕Never input confidential personal data into Gemini. Do not paste names, addresses, ID numbers, financial account details, or any personally identifiable information of CBF staff, grantees, or beneficiaries.
✕Never share confidential CBF strategy, financial, or legal documents with Gemini without checking with your manager first. Internal CBF documents that are not meant for public distribution should not be pasted into AI tools.
✕Never treat Gemini output as fact without verification. Gemini can generate plausible but incorrect information — especially for specific statistics, dates, or technical details. Always verify factual claims before including them in official CBF reports or communications.
✕Never send AI-generated content without reviewing and editing it. All CBF communications — emails, reports, proposals — must be reviewed and personalised by the staff member responsible. Unedited AI output does not meet CBF's professional standards.
✕Never use Gemini to make decisions. Gemini is a tool to support your thinking, not replace it. All project, financial, and strategic decisions remain the responsibility of CBF staff, not AI tools.
When in doubt, ask your manager. If you are unsure whether a particular use of Gemini is appropriate — especially involving sensitive documents or partner information — check with your manager before proceeding. CBF's data protection and confidentiality obligations apply to AI tools just as they do to any other system.
Best Practices

Tips for CBF staff

Habits that make Gemini a genuinely useful working tool rather than a novelty.

✍️

Use Gemini for first drafts

Gemini excels at generating starting points — a rough draft, an outline, a list of ideas. Your job is then to edit, personalise, and improve. This is almost always faster than writing from scratch.

🔄

Iterate — don't restart

If the first response is not right, follow up in the same conversation. Ask it to be shorter, more formal, or to add a specific detail. You rarely need to start a new conversation from scratch.

🌍

Add CBF context to every prompt

Gemini does not know CBF unless you tell it. Including a brief context line — "CBF is a Caribbean conservation fund working across 14 countries" — significantly improves the relevance of every response.

✅

Verify facts and figures

Gemini can confidently state things that are incorrect. Any statistic, citation, or specific fact generated by Gemini should be independently verified before appearing in a CBF report or presentation.

🔀

Use Gemini + NotebookLM together

A powerful combination: use NotebookLM to extract accurate findings from source documents, then use Gemini to help write those findings into polished report language. Each tool does what it does best.

📋

Build a personal prompt library

Keep a simple Google Doc with your most useful Gemini prompts — the ones that consistently produce good results for your role. Reusing and refining proven prompts is much faster than writing new ones every time.
